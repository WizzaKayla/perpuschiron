Hidden Beach of Bali

A beach is a stretch of land consisting of sand or gravel that borders the sea. This area is formed from the deposition of rock material, gravel, and sand carried by rivers or eroded by waves. While some Bali beaches are world-renowned, like the bustling Kuta and Jimbaran, there are also many hidden gems waiting to be discovered. These secluded beaches offer tranquility, stunning scenery, and a chance to escape the crowds.


Biodata

Name: Green Bowl Beach

Location: Ungasan, Bali, Indonesia

In Bali there is Green Bowl Beach, a secluded paradise. This narrow stretch of coarse white sand, only 30 meters wide, sits at the base of a 75 meter high cliff. To reach it, you have to climb down hundreds of steep steps, but that's part of its charm. These adventures keep visitors away from the crowds, attracting only weekend tourists and intrepid surfers who crave quiet waves and exotic escapes. Adding to its mystique are two large limestone caves located on the shore. This natural sanctuary is home to a small rock temple and offers shade and protection for beachgoers and surfers looking for a break from the exhilarating waves. So, Green Bowl Beach is not just a beautiful stretch of sand, it's an adventure waiting to be discovered.
